Flag 5

Hashing and operational security

Instructions:

Using secure passwords is a cyber security essential. When websites use passwords, they are usually hashed to ensure privacy, however there are databases that exist which can be used to brute force insecure passwords.

Below are 4 MD5 (hashing algorithm) hashes. If any of these passwords were leaked, even if hashed, they could be cracked due to their simplicity!

  • 482c811da5d5b4bc6d497ffa98491e38
  • 25f9e794323b453885f5181f1b624d0b
  • 7ac66c0f148de9519b8bd264312c4d64
  • c7a6002549b0ff54324ecce62cd9ab6d
For this challenge, the flag is the first character of the reversed MD5 hashes joined with the next. EG. if the reverse of the above hashes were:
  • password
  • mypassword
  • qwertyuiop
  • 987654321
The flag would be flag{pmq9}.

Your task is to reverse engineer the 4 MD5 hashes above.
